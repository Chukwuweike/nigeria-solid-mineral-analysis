Nigeria Solid Minerals Sector Analysis (2022–2023)

A data-driven snapshot of Nigeria's solid minerals production, export orientation, and global market positioning — built from official government and international geological survey data.

## Objective

To understand the structure, export orientation, and global relevance of Nigeria's solid minerals sector using 2022–2023 data, and to identify where the sector's strategic opportunities and structural weaknesses lie.

## Data Sources

- **NEITI Annual Reports (2022 & 2023)** — Nigeria Extractive Industries Transparency Initiative, providing national production and export volumes by mineral
- **World Mineral Production 2019–2023** — British Geological Survey (BGS), providing global production figures for comparison

## Scope

18 minerals selected based on data availability, strategic relevance, and Nigeria's stated sector interests: barite, beryl, coal, columbite, copper, feldspar, gold, gypsum, iron ore, kaolin, lead/zinc ore, limestone, lithium, manganese, mica, talc, tantalite, and tin ore.

## Tools

Microsoft Excel — data cleaning, KPI derivation, and charting

## Key Derived KPIs

- **Export Intensity (EI)** — ratio of export volume to production volume for a given mineral and year, categorized as No Export, Low, Medium, High, or Above Unity
- **Global Share** — ratio of Nigeria's production to global production for a given mineral, categorized as Negligible, Low, Moderate, or Significant

## Key Findings

- Limestone dominates Nigeria's mineral production by volume but is almost entirely consumed domestically, supporting the infrastructure and construction sectors
- Lead/zinc ore, lithium, manganese, and columbite are strongly export-oriented, with export volumes high relative to production
- Nigeria's lithium production rose over 50% between 2022 and 2023, tracking a ~30% rise in global lithium demand — and Nigeria's global lithium production share sits at roughly 37%, a genuinely significant position
- Despite pockets of export intensity, only 19% of mineral production categories register a "Significant" global market share — Nigeria largely participates in global mineral markets without influencing them
- The sector shows minimal downstream processing before export, meaning value capture happens largely outside Nigeria

## The Core Insight

Nigeria mines minerals the world increasingly wants, and responds to global demand signals — but exports them as raw ore rather than processed material, capturing a fraction of their eventual value. The opportunity is not more extraction; it's more processing.

## Limitations

This analysis is based on a two-year window (2022–2023), which limits long-term trend analysis. Classification differences between NEITI and BGS datasets (e.g. lead/zinc reported jointly vs. separately) introduce some uncertainty in global comparisons. No price, value, firm-level, or mine-level data is included — findings are volume-based only.
